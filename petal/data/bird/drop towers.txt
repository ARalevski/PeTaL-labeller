Fluid shift versus body size: changes of hematological parameters and body fluid volume in hindlimb-unloaded mice, rats and rabbits
<p><bold>Summary:</bold> Allometric investigation of hematological parameters and body fluid volume changes during simulated <strong><span style="color:yellowgreen">micrograv</span></strong>ity reveals that red blood cell count decrease is size independent, while extracellular fluid volume increase is proportional to body size.</p>
